# Platform Cadence Guide

Recommended publishing cadence by platform, with reasoning for each recommendation.

---

## X / Twitter

**Recommended cadence**: 1-3 posts per day

**Reasoning**: X's algorithmic timeline rewards frequency. Content has a short half-life (15-45 minutes for most accounts). Regular posting increases the chance of catching different audience segments across time zones. However, every post must still be worth reading -- never post just to hit a number.

**Content mix**:
- 2-3 original tweets or short observations per day
- 1 thread per week (or 2 if you have enough material)
- Engagement replies and quote tweets do not count toward the cadence -- they are separate and should happen organically

**What to avoid**: Posting 5+ times a day with filler content. Audiences notice when you are padding the schedule.

---

## LinkedIn

**Recommended cadence**: 3-5 posts per week

**Reasoning**: LinkedIn's algorithm heavily favors consistent posters. Content has a longer half-life than X (12-48 hours), so each post has more time to gain traction. The algorithm also rewards engagement in the first 60-90 minutes, so timing matters. Post when your audience is active (typically 7-9am or 12-1pm in their timezone on weekdays).

**Content mix**:
- 2-3 text-based posts per week (insights, lessons, observations)
- 1 longer narrative or story per week
- 1 engagement post (question, poll, or commentary on an industry trend)

**What to avoid**: Posting on weekends (dramatically lower reach for most B2B audiences). Using LinkedIn as a blog mirror (repurpose, do not repost).

---

## Blog

**Recommended cadence**: 1-2 posts per week

**Reasoning**: Blog posts are higher effort and higher value than social posts. Quality matters more than frequency for SEO and audience trust. One well-researched, well-written post per week builds authority faster than three rushed posts. Blog content also has the longest shelf life -- a good post can drive traffic for years.

**Content mix**:
- 1 substantial post per week (1500+ words, deeply useful)
- 1 shorter post or update if capacity allows (800-1200 words)
- Monthly: 1 cornerstone/pillar piece (2500+ words, comprehensive)

**What to avoid**: Publishing for the sake of publishing. An empty week is better than a weak post that dilutes your brand.

---

## Newsletter

**Recommended cadence**: 1 per week

**Reasoning**: Weekly newsletters build habit and expectation. Subscribers know when to expect you in their inbox, and the rhythm creates a relationship. Biweekly or monthly newsletters are harder to build habit around and easier to forget about. Daily newsletters require significant production capacity and only work for news-focused or curation-focused formats.

**Content mix**:
- 1 main insight, story, or analysis per issue
- 2-3 curated links with commentary
- 1 personal note or observation
- 1 CTA (but rotate what you are asking for)

**What to avoid**: Skipping weeks without notice (breaks trust). Sending more than 2 per week unless your audience explicitly signed up for daily content.

---

## YouTube

**Recommended cadence**: 1 video per week

**Reasoning**: YouTube rewards consistency heavily. The algorithm learns your posting schedule and surfaces your content to subscribers accordingly. Weekly uploads are the minimum for channel growth. However, video production is time-intensive, so plan production capacity carefully. One good video per week beats two rushed ones.

**Content mix**:
- 1 main video per week (8-15 minutes is the current sweet spot for most niches)
- 1-2 YouTube Shorts per week (optional, but helps with discovery)
- Community posts between uploads to maintain engagement

**What to avoid**: Inconsistent upload schedules (confuses the algorithm and your audience). Sacrificing production quality for frequency.

---

## Reddit

**Recommended cadence**: 2-3 posts per week

**Reasoning**: Reddit is community-first. Posting must feel genuine and valuable, not promotional. The cadence should be dictated by how often you have something genuinely useful to contribute. Subreddit rules vary widely -- some allow self-promotion sparingly, others ban it entirely. Always provide standalone value.

**Content mix**:
- 1-2 helpful comments or responses in relevant subreddits per day
- 1 original post per week in a relevant subreddit (advice, insight, or useful resource)
- 1 cross-post or share of your content (when subreddit rules allow and it is genuinely relevant)

**What to avoid**: Drive-by link dropping. Reddit communities are hostile to transparent self-promotion. Contribute first, share later.

---

## Podcast

**Recommended cadence**: 1 episode per week (or every two weeks)

**Reasoning**: Podcast audiences are habitual listeners. They subscribe expecting a regular cadence. Weekly episodes build momentum and keep you in the listener's routine. Biweekly works if production is a constraint, but weekly is the growth-optimized cadence.

**Content mix**:
- 1 main episode per week (20-45 minutes depending on format)
- Occasional bonus episodes for timely topics or special guests
- Companion social posts to promote each episode

**What to avoid**: Irregular release schedules. Podcast audiences will churn if they cannot rely on your cadence.

---

## General Principles

1. **Start conservative**: It is easier to increase cadence than to decrease it. Start with the minimum viable cadence and increase once you have a reliable production pipeline.

2. **Quality over frequency, always**: Reducing cadence to maintain quality is the right call every time. Your audience remembers bad content longer than they notice a missed post.

3. **Batch production**: Produce content in batches (e.g., write all social posts for the week in one session). This is more efficient and creates more consistent quality.

4. **Track and adjust**: After 4-6 weeks, review engagement data. If increasing cadence did not increase engagement, scale back. If certain days or times perform better, adjust the schedule.

5. **Seasonal adjustment**: Content consumption patterns shift around holidays, summer, and industry events. Plan lighter cadence during low-attention periods and heavier cadence during peak interest windows.
