# Hook Patterns for X/Twitter

Proven hook formulas organized by type. Each pattern includes the formula and a concrete example.

---

## 1. Curiosity Gap

**Formula:** Hint at a surprising outcome without revealing it.

**Examples:**
- "I mass-deleted 80% of our microservices. Here's what happened to our uptime."
- "We switched from React to vanilla JS on our largest app. The results surprised everyone."
- "I asked 50 CTOs the same question. Only 3 gave the same answer."

---

## 2. Bold Claim

**Formula:** State a strong opinion that will make people stop and react.

**Examples:**
- "Most startups die because they ship too many features, not too few."
- "Your CI/CD pipeline is probably costing you 10x what it should."
- "The best engineers I've hired had no computer science degree."

---

## 3. Stat Shock

**Formula:** Lead with a number that surprises or challenges assumptions.

**Examples:**
- "93% of A/B tests at our company showed no significant difference. We ran 2,000 of them."
- "The average developer spends 41% of their time reading code, not writing it."
- "We reduced our AWS bill from $48k/mo to $6k/mo with one architectural change."

---

## 4. Story Hook

**Formula:** Drop the reader into the middle of a specific moment.

**Examples:**
- "At 2am on a Friday, our on-call engineer got paged. The database was empty."
- "My first startup failed in 8 months. Here's the exact mistake I made on day 1."
- "Last week a junior developer on my team found a bug that had been in production for 3 years."

---

## 5. Contrarian Take

**Formula:** Directly challenge popular wisdom or common practice.

**Examples:**
- "Unpopular opinion: code reviews slow your team down more than they help."
- "Everyone tells you to fail fast. That's terrible advice."
- "Clean code is overrated. Shipping code is underrated."

---

## 6. Listicle Promise

**Formula:** Promise a specific number of actionable items.

**Examples:**
- "7 terminal commands I use every single day that most developers don't know about:"
- "5 pricing mistakes that killed otherwise great SaaS products:"
- "10 things I learned from reading 200 postmortems:"

---

## 7. "Here's What Nobody Tells You About..."

**Formula:** Promise insider knowledge or hidden truth.

**Examples:**
- "Here's what nobody tells you about raising a Series A:"
- "Nobody talks about the worst part of being a senior engineer:"
- "Here's what actually happens when your app goes viral on HN:"

---

## 8. Before/After

**Formula:** Show a transformation with concrete details.

**Examples:**
- "6 months ago, our deploys took 45 minutes. Today they take 90 seconds. Here's what we changed:"
- "Last year I was mass-applying to jobs with zero callbacks. This year I got 12 offers. Here's what changed:"

---

## 9. Pattern Interrupt

**Formula:** Say something unexpected that breaks the reader's scroll pattern.

**Examples:**
- "Stop writing tests."
- "Delete your backlog."
- "The worst code I've ever written is in production right now. It handles $2M/day."

---

## 10. Question Hook

**Formula:** Ask a question the reader genuinely wants to answer or see answered.

**Examples:**
- "What's the one tool you couldn't live without as a developer?"
- "If you could mass-delete one thing from your codebase, what would it be?"
- "What's the most expensive bug you've ever shipped?"

---

## 11. "I Was Wrong About..."

**Formula:** Admit a past mistake or changed opinion. Authenticity stops the scroll.

**Examples:**
- "I spent years arguing against TypeScript. I was completely wrong."
- "I used to think standups were essential. After removing them, our team shipped faster."

---

## 12. "The Real Reason..."

**Formula:** Promise the underlying explanation behind something visible.

**Examples:**
- "The real reason your open source project isn't getting contributors:"
- "The real reason senior developers say 'it depends':"

---

## 13. "I Spent {Time} Doing {Thing}"

**Formula:** Lead with invested effort as proof of authority.

**Examples:**
- "I spent 6 months building a SaaS no one wanted. Here are the warning signs I ignored:"
- "I spent 100 hours reading Kubernetes source code. Here's what I found:"

---

## 14. Results-First

**Formula:** Lead with the end result, then explain the path.

**Examples:**
- "We grew from 0 to 10k users in 3 weeks with $0 in marketing spend."
- "Our error rate dropped 99.7% after a single config change."

---

## 15. Hot Take + Qualification

**Formula:** Strong opinion followed by a nuance that shows you've thought it through.

**Examples:**
- "Kubernetes is overkill for 95% of companies. The other 5% can't live without it. Here's how to know which you are:"
- "AI won't replace developers. But developers who use AI will replace those who don't. Here's why:"

---

## 16. Direct Address

**Formula:** Call out a specific audience and speak directly to them.

**Examples:**
- "If you're building a developer tool, stop reading. This thread will save you 6 months."
- "Founders who bootstrapped past $1M ARR: this is for you."

---

## 17. Time-Bound Discovery

**Formula:** Attach the insight to a recent, specific moment.

**Examples:**
- "Last week I discovered a PostgreSQL feature that has existed since version 9.4 and nobody uses it."
- "Yesterday I watched a Google engineer debug a production issue in real time. Here's what I learned:"

---

## Anti-Patterns: Hooks That Fail

Do not use these:
- "Thread:" with no hook (just a label, not a reason to read)
- "I've been thinking a lot about..." (so has everyone; be specific)
- "Quick thread on..." (never call your own content "quick" -- let the reader judge)
- "Let me explain..." (just explain)
- "Okay so..." (filler)
- "Hot take:" followed by a lukewarm opinion
