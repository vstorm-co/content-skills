# 3D Render Style

## Description

Smooth, polished 3D renders with soft lighting and clean surfaces. Think product photography but generated -- studio-quality shots of objects, scenes, or abstract compositions rendered in 3D. The aesthetic leans toward Apple product pages, Stripe illustrations, or Figma marketing visuals.

---

## Key Characteristics

- **Lighting**: Soft, diffused studio lighting. Subtle shadows with soft edges. Often a key light with gentle fill.
- **Surfaces**: Smooth, clean materials. Matte or slightly glossy. No visible texture grain unless intentional.
- **Perspective**: Isometric or subtle perspective. Consistent depth across the scene.
- **Colors**: Limited palette, often pastel or muted. Backgrounds are clean (white, light gray, or single soft color).
- **Backdrop**: Studio-like environment. Seamless background or subtle gradient. No busy scenes.
- **Detail**: Medium to high. Objects are recognizable and detailed, but the overall composition is clean and uncluttered.

## Sub-Styles

### Clay Render
A popular variant where everything looks like it is made of soft clay or Play-Doh. Adds a friendly, approachable feel.

Additional keywords: "clay render, clay material, soft plastic, rounded edges, toy-like, miniature, pastel colors"

### Glass / Translucent
Modern trend where objects have frosted glass or translucent materials.

Additional keywords: "frosted glass, translucent, glass morphism, refractive, light passing through, ethereal"

### Isometric Scene
Flat isometric perspective (no vanishing points) showing a scene from above.

Additional keywords: "isometric, top-down 45 degrees, no perspective distortion, flat lighting, game-like, miniature world"

## When to Use

- Product landing pages and feature highlights
- SaaS marketing visuals
- App store screenshots and mockups
- Presentation slides where flat illustration feels too simple
- Abstract concept visualization (data, cloud, AI)
- Social media graphics for premium/polished brands

## When Not to Use

- When authenticity and human connection matter (use editorial photography)
- When the brand is scrappy or grassroots (3D renders feel polished/corporate)
- When the content is narrative or storytelling (use film still)
- When quick iteration is needed (3D prompts require more tuning)
