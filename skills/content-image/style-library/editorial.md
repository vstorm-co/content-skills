# Editorial Photography Style

## Description

Clean, professional photography that would fit in a magazine editorial or high-quality publication. Natural and authentic feeling, but carefully composed. The opposite of stock photography -- every image should feel like it captures a real moment with intentional framing.

---

## Key Characteristics

- **Lighting**: Natural light preferred. Window light, golden hour, or overcast sky. Avoid harsh direct flash.
- **Depth of field**: Shallow to medium. Subject sharp, background softly blurred. f/2.0 to f/4.0 equivalent.
- **Color grading**: Muted, slightly desaturated. Warm or neutral tones. No oversaturation.
- **Composition**: Rule of thirds or centered with intent. Negative space used deliberately.
- **Subject interaction**: Candid or quasi-candid. Subjects appear natural, not posed for camera.
- **Lens**: 35mm to 85mm equivalent. Avoid wide-angle distortion on faces.

---

## When to Use

- Blog hero images
- About page team photos
- Case study illustrations
- Newsletter headers
- Professional social media posts (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Company culture pieces

## When Not to Use

- Technical diagrams or data visualization (use infographic)
- Bold social media graphics (use flat illustration)
- Product catalog shots (use 3D render)
- Cinematic or dramatic content (use film still)

---

## Negative Prompt Elements

"stock photo, posed, smiling at camera, thumbs up, handshake, generic office, clip art, oversaturated, HDR, flash photography, wide angle distortion, fish eye"
