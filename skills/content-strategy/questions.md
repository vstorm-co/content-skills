# Strategy Questions

Ask these questions before developing a content strategy. Not every question applies to every situation. Use judgment about which to ask explicitly, which to infer from context, and which to skip.

---

## Goal Questions

- **What is the business goal?** Are we trying to generate leads, build brand awareness, establish authority, drive product adoption, recruit talent, or something else?
- **What does success look like?** How will we know the content strategy is working? What metrics matter?
- **What is the timeline?** Is this a 30-day sprint, a quarterly plan, or an ongoing editorial strategy?
- **What has been tried before?** What content has the team published previously? What worked? What did not?

## Audience Questions

- **Who is the primary audience?** Be specific -- role, seniority, industry, company size.
- **Who is the secondary audience?** Is there a second group that matters but is not the primary target?
- **What do they already believe?** About the topic, the industry, and the brand.
- **What should they believe after consuming our content?** What is the belief shift we are trying to create?
- **Where do they currently get information?** Which publications, newsletters, social accounts, podcasts, or communities?
- **What content format do they prefer?** Long-form articles, short threads, video, podcasts, newsletters?
- **What are their biggest pain points?** Related to the topic at hand.
- **What language do they use?** Jargon level, formality, cultural references.

## Competitive Questions

- **What are competitors publishing?** What topics, formats, and cadence?
- **What is everyone saying?** What is the consensus view in the space?
- **What is nobody saying?** Where is the gap? What contrarian or underexplored angle exists?
- **What can we say that nobody else can?** Unique data, experience, perspective, or access.
- **What is the audience tired of hearing?** Overplayed topics or angles to avoid.

## Positioning Questions

- **What is our unique angle?** Why should someone consume our content instead of anyone else's?
- **What is our expertise?** What do we know better than most? What have we built or experienced?
- **What tone fits our brand?** Serious? Playful? Technical? Conversational? Provocative?
- **What is our hot take?** What do we believe that most people in the space disagree with?
- **What story are we telling over time?** Is there a narrative arc across our content, or is it episodic?

## Resource Questions

- **Who is creating the content?** One person? A team? External contributors?
- **What is the production capacity?** How many pieces per week/month is realistic?
- **What assets do we have?** Data, case studies, customer stories, internal experts, research.
- **What platforms do we own?** Blog, newsletter, social accounts, YouTube channel, podcast.
- **What is the budget?** For tools, promotion, design, freelancers, ads.

## Distribution Questions

- **Where does the audience already spend time?** Which platforms, communities, and events?
- **What is our organic reach?** Current follower counts, email list size, site traffic.
- **Do we have a paid distribution budget?** For social ads, sponsorships, or newsletter placements.
- **Who are potential amplifiers?** People or accounts that might share or engage with our content.
- **What is our SEO situation?** Domain authority, existing rankings, keyword opportunities.

---

## How to Use These Questions

1. **Do not ask all of them in a dump.** Start with the 3-4 most critical questions based on what you already know. Fill in the rest from context or follow-up naturally.
2. **Infer when you can.** If the user says "I run a DevTools startup targeting senior engineers," you already have answers to several audience questions. Do not re-ask what is obvious.
3. **Prioritize gaps.** Focus your questions on the areas where you have the least information and the most impact on strategy quality.
4. **Treat answers as hypotheses.** Strategy is iterative. The first pass may need revision as the user provides feedback.
