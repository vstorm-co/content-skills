# Brand Identity Questions — Phase 1

These are the detailed question scripts for the Identity phase of brand onboarding. Claude should not read these questions verbatim as a list. Use them as a guide, adapt phrasing to the conversation, and skip questions whose answers have already been revealed.

---

## Q1: Brand Name

**Ask:** "Let's start with the basics. What's the name of your brand or company?"

**Follow-up if needed:**
- "Is that the legal name, the trade name, or both?"
- "Any common misspellings or alternate names people use?"

**Extract:** Official brand name, any variations, correct capitalization.

---

## Q2: What You Do

**Ask:** "In plain language, what does [brand name] actually do? Pretend I know nothing about your industry."

**Probe for vagueness:**
- If they say "we help businesses grow" -> "How, specifically? What does a customer hire you to do?"
- If they say "we're a platform for X" -> "Walk me through what happens when someone signs up. What's the first thing they do?"
- If they use jargon -> "Explain that like I'm a smart person who's never heard of your industry."

**Extract:** A concrete description of the product or service. What the customer gets. What changes in their life after using it.

---

## Q3: Who You Serve

**Ask:** "Who is your ideal customer? Not the broadest possible audience, but the person who gets the most value from what you do."

**Probe for specifics:**
- If they say "developers" -> "Which developers? Frontend? Backend? What seniority? What stack? Working at what kind of company?"
- If they say "small businesses" -> "What kind? A coffee shop is a small business. So is a 50-person SaaS company. Who specifically?"
- If they say "everyone" -> "If you could only serve one type of person for the next year, who would it be?"

**Extract:** Role/title, experience level, industry, company size, primary pain point. See `audience.md` for the full audience deep-dive if more detail is needed.

---

## Q4: Elevator Pitch

**Ask:** "If you had 30 seconds in an elevator with your dream customer, what would you say?"

**Probe:**
- If it's longer than 30 seconds -> "That's great context, but let's compress it. What's the one sentence version?"
- If it's too abstract -> "I want to feel the benefit. What would make them say 'tell me more'?"
- If it sounds like everyone else -> "Could a competitor say the exact same thing? If yes, what makes yours different?"

**Extract:** A tight 1-2 sentence pitch that communicates the core value proposition.

---

## Q5: Competitive Landscape

**Ask:** "Who do your customers compare you to? Or who would they use if you didn't exist?"

**Probe:**
- "What do those alternatives get right?"
- "What do they get wrong?"
- "When someone switches from [competitor] to you, what's the moment they realize the difference?"

**Extract:** 2-5 named competitors or alternative approaches. Honest assessment of their strengths and weaknesses.

---

## Q6: Differentiator

**Ask:** "What's the single biggest reason someone picks you over the alternatives?"

**Probe hard here -- this is the most important question:**
- If they say "better quality" -> "Quality how? Faster? More reliable? Better designed? Give me a specific example."
- If they say "customer service" -> "What does your customer service do that others don't? Can you give me a story?"
- If they say "price" -> "Are you the cheapest? If not, what justifies the premium?"
- If they say "innovation" -> "What's the most recent thing you shipped that no one else has?"

**Test:** Can a competitor truthfully make the same claim? If yes, it is not a differentiator. Push deeper.

**Extract:** A specific, falsifiable differentiator. Something a competitor could not copy-paste into their own marketing.

---

## Q7: One-Word Essence

**Ask:** "If you had to distill your brand down to a single word -- the feeling someone should get every time they interact with you -- what would it be?"

**Probe:**
- If they struggle -> "Think about your best customer interaction ever. What word describes that feeling?"
- If they give two words -> "I'm going to make you pick one. Which matters more?"
- Common answers that need sharpening: "quality" (too vague), "trust" (too generic), "innovation" (overused). Push them to something more distinctive.

**Extract:** A single word that acts as a gut-check filter for all brand decisions.

---

## What Good Looks Like

After Phase 1, you should be able to write a paragraph like this without guessing:

> "[Brand] is a [what it is] that helps [specific audience] [achieve specific outcome]. Unlike [competitors], [Brand] [specific differentiator]. The core feeling is [one-word essence]."

If you cannot fill in every bracket with confidence, you need to ask more questions.
