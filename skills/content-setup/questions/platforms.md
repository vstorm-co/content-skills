# Platform & Distribution Questions — Phase 4

These questions capture where the brand publishes content and how often. This information drives content format, length, and scheduling recommendations across all content skills.

---

## Q1: Current Platforms

**Ask:** "Where do you currently publish content? List everything, even if it's inconsistent."

**Walk through each category:**

**Owned platforms:**
- Blog or website
- Newsletter / email list
- Documentation / knowledge base
- Podcast
- YouTube or video channel

**Social platforms:**
- Twitter / X
- LinkedIn (personal, company page, or both)
- Instagram
- TikTok
- Threads
- Mastodon / Bluesky
- Reddit (subreddit, comments, or both)

**Community platforms:**
- Discord server
- Slack community
- GitHub discussions
- Forum or community site

**Third-party platforms:**
- Medium
- Dev.to
- Hashnode
- Substack
- Industry publications (guest posts)

**Probe:**
- "Which of these do you actually enjoy creating for vs. which feel like obligations?"
- "Which platform drives the most meaningful engagement (not just vanity metrics)?"
- "Any platforms you've tried and abandoned? What went wrong?"

**Extract:** Complete list of active platforms, engagement quality assessment, abandoned platforms and reasons.

---

## Q2: Frequency Goals

**Ask:** "How often do you want to publish on each platform? Be realistic, not aspirational."

**Probe for reality:**
- "What's your current cadence? Are you hitting it?"
- "If you could only publish on one platform consistently, which would it be?"
- "Who's creating the content? Just you, or do you have a team?"
- "How much time per week can you realistically spend on content?"

**Common frequency patterns:**
- Blog: 1-4 posts per month
- Newsletter: Weekly or biweekly
- Twitter/X: Daily or multiple times daily
- LinkedIn: 2-5 posts per week
- Instagram: 3-7 posts per week
- Podcast: Weekly or biweekly
- YouTube: Weekly or biweekly

**Probe if frequencies seem unsustainable:**
- "That's ambitious. With your current resources, do you think you can sustain that for 6 months?"
- "What would you cut if you had to drop one platform tomorrow?"

**Extract:** Target frequency per platform, current vs. desired cadence, resource constraints, priority ranking.

---

## Q3: Content Types Per Platform

**Ask:** "What types of content do you create for each platform? For example, your blog might be tutorials and case studies, while your Twitter is quick tips and industry commentary."

**Content type categories to explore:**

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Tutorials / How-tos | Step-by-step instructional content |
| Case studies | Customer success stories |
| Thought leadership | Original perspectives on industry trends |
| Product updates | Feature announcements, changelogs |
| Behind-the-scenes | Process, culture, building in public |
| Curated / Commentary | Sharing and commenting on others' content |
| Data / Research | Original data, surveys, benchmarks |
| Interviews | Conversations with customers, experts, team |
| Quick tips | Short, actionable advice |
| Threads / Series | Multi-part connected content |
| Video scripts | Scripted content for video production |
| Community responses | Engaging in discussions, answering questions |

**Probe:**
- "Which content type performs best for you?"
- "Which type is easiest for you to create?"
- "Is there a type you've never tried but want to?"

**Extract:** Content types mapped to platforms, performance assessment, creation difficulty, aspirational types.

---

## Q4: Platforms to Avoid

**Ask:** "Are there platforms you explicitly do not want to be on? Or places where your brand should never appear?"

**Probe:**
- "Is this a strategic choice or a resource constraint?"
- "If someone offered to manage that platform for you for free, would you say yes?"
- "Any platforms where being present could actually hurt your brand?"

**Common reasons to avoid:**
- Audience mismatch (e.g., B2B company avoiding TikTok)
- Brand risk (platform reputation, content adjacency)
- Resource constraints (no bandwidth for video)
- Strategic focus (better to be great on 2 platforms than mediocre on 8)
- Past negative experience

**Extract:** Excluded platforms with clear reasoning. Distinguish between "never" and "not yet."

## Content Pillars (Bonus)

If the conversation naturally leads here, introduce content pillars:

**Ask:** "Let's define 3-5 content pillars -- the core topics you always come back to. What subjects does your brand have the right to speak about?"

**Probe:**
- "What topics could you write 20 articles about without running out of ideas?"
- "What does your audience come to you specifically to learn about?"
- "What topics would feel off-brand or out of scope?"

**Examples:**
- A developer tools company might have pillars like: Developer Productivity, Engineering Culture, Technical Deep Dives, Open Source, Product Updates.
- A design agency might have: Design Process, Client Collaboration, Industry Trends, Case Studies, Design Systems.

**Extract:** 3-5 named content pillars with a one-sentence description of each.
