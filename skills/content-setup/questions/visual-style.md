# Visual Style Questions — Phase 3

These questions document the brand's visual identity. Claude is not designing anything -- just capturing what exists or what the user aspires to. This information feeds into content creation when visuals, formatting, or brand-consistent styling is needed.

---

## Q1: Logo

**Ask:** "Tell me about your logo. What does it look like, and are there rules about how it should be used?"

**Probe:**
- "Is it a wordmark, an icon, or a combination?"
- "Does it have a symbol or icon that works on its own (like for a favicon or social avatar)?"
- "Any specific clear-space rules, minimum sizes, or placement restrictions?"
- "Are there versions you should never use? (e.g., never on a dark background, never stretched)"

**If they don't have a logo yet:**
- "That's fine. Let's skip the logo specifics for now and focus on the visual direction you're going for."

**Extract:** Logo type, usage rules, variations available, restrictions.

---

## Q2: Color Palette

**Ask:** "What are your brand colors? If you have hex codes, that's ideal, but descriptions work too."

**Probe for structure:**
- "Which color is the primary one -- the one that should dominate most content?"
- "Do you have secondary or accent colors?"
- "What about background colors? Do you typically go light, dark, or mixed?"
- "Are there colors you explicitly avoid?"

**If they know hex values:**
- Capture them precisely. Ask for the full set: primary, secondary, accent, background, text.

**If they don't have a defined palette:**
- "What colors feel right for your brand? Think about the emotion you want to evoke."
- "Look at your website or product right now. What colors do you see most?"
- "Any colors that would feel completely wrong?"

**Extract:** Primary color (hex if available), secondary colors, accent colors, background preference, colors to avoid.

---

## Q3: Reference Brands

**Ask:** "Are there brands whose visual style you admire? Not necessarily competitors -- any brand whose look and feel resonates."

**Probe:**
- "What specifically do you like? Is it the color choices, the typography, the photography, the overall vibe?"
- "Is there a brand that looks exactly like what you don't want?"
- "If someone confused your brand's visual style with another company's, who would you want it to be confused with?"

**Extract:** 2-4 reference brands with specific visual attributes admired. Anti-references (brands to avoid looking like).

---

## Q4: Typography

**Ask:** "What's your relationship with fonts? Do you have specific brand typefaces, or is it more of a general preference?"

**Explore:**
- **Serif vs. sans-serif**: "Do you lean toward classic/editorial (serif like Times, Georgia, Playfair) or modern/clean (sans-serif like Inter, Helvetica, DM Sans)?"
- **Heading vs. body**: "Do headings and body text use the same font, or different ones?"
- **Personality**: "Should your type feel bold and loud, quiet and elegant, techy and minimal, or something else?"
- **Specific fonts**: "Are there specific fonts you already use? If so, what are they?"

**If they have brand fonts:**
- Capture exact font names, weights used, and where each is applied.

**If they don't:**
- "What category feels right?" Offer examples:
  - Clean and modern: Inter, DM Sans, Outfit
  - Editorial and refined: Playfair Display, Lora, Source Serif
  - Bold and expressive: Syne, Space Grotesk, Cabinet Grotesk
  - Monospace/techy: JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono, Fira Code

**Extract:** Font names (or preferences), heading vs. body distinction, weight preferences, personality descriptors.

---

## Q5: Photography & Illustration Style

**Ask:** "When your content includes images, what style fits your brand?"

**Dimensions to explore:**

**Photos vs. Illustrations vs. Abstract:**
- "Do you use real photography, illustrations, abstract graphics, or a mix?"
- "Are stock photos acceptable, or does everything need to be original?"

**Photography style (if applicable):**
- Candid vs. posed
- Bright and airy vs. moody and dramatic
- People-focused vs. product-focused vs. environmental
- Filtered/stylized vs. natural

**Illustration style (if applicable):**
- Flat vs. dimensional
- Minimal line art vs. detailed
- Geometric vs. organic
- Character-driven vs. abstract

**Probe:**
- "Show me (or describe) an image that feels very on-brand."
- "What kind of image would feel completely wrong on your website?"
- "Do you have an existing image library or brand photography?"

**Extract:** Primary visual medium (photo/illustration/abstract), style descriptors, what to avoid, any existing asset libraries.

---

## When Visual Identity Is Minimal

Some brands, especially early-stage or developer-focused ones, have very little visual identity defined. That's fine. In this case:

1. Capture whatever preferences exist, even informal ones.
2. Note the gaps explicitly in `brand/VISUAL.md` so they can be filled later.
3. Focus on what they want to avoid -- anti-preferences are often clearer than aspirations.
4. Suggest they look at the reference brands section and revisit when they're ready.

Do not invent visual identity that doesn't exist. Document reality, not aspiration (unless the user explicitly wants to define aspirational guidelines).
