# Pattern: Podcast Content to Social Media Posts

## When to Use

The source is a podcast episode (transcript, notes, or audio) and the targets are social media posts across X, LinkedIn, and other platforms.

---

## Transformation Steps

### Step 1: Mine the Transcript

Podcasts are rich sources of content, but most of the transcript is conversational filler. Mine for:

**Quotable moments**: Short, punchy statements that stand alone. Look for:
- Strong opinions ("The truth is, most startups don't have a product problem. They have a distribution problem.")
- Surprising claims backed by experience ("We tested 14 onboarding flows before finding one that actually converted.")
- Frameworks or mental models ("I think about content strategy in three layers: audience, angle, and amplification.")
- Contrarian takes ("Controversial opinion: your engineering blog shouldn't be written by engineers.")

**Story beats**: Narratives that can be condensed into social-length stories:
- Origin stories ("When we first started, we were literally emailing CSV files back and forth...")
- Failure stories ("We spent six months building a feature nobody wanted. Here's how we figured that out.")
- Turning points ("The moment everything changed was when a customer told us...")

**Data and numbers**: Specific metrics, results, or statistics mentioned in conversation.

**Practical advice**: Actionable tips that can stand alone as useful content.

### Step 2: Create an Extraction Sheet

For each promising extract, document:

| Extract | Type | Best Platform | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Quote or paraphrase] | Quote / Story / Data / Tip | X / LinkedIn / Both | [Any additional context needed] |

Aim for 8-15 extracts per episode. Not all will become posts -- this is the raw material.

### Step 3: Craft Platform-Specific Posts

#### For X / Twitter

**Quote posts**: Take a quotable moment and frame it as a standalone tweet.
```
"Most startups don't have a product problem. They have a distribution problem."

-- @GuestName on the latest episode of [Podcast Name]

The full conversation: [link]
```

**Insight threads**: Take a key story or framework from the episode and expand it into a 3-7 tweet thread with your own commentary.

**Audiogram suggestions**: Identify 30-60 second clips that would work as audio snippets with a waveform visual. Note the timestamp range for the audio editor.

#### For LinkedIn

**Story posts**: Take a narrative beat from the podcast and expand it into a LinkedIn story format:
```
My guest @Name shared something that changed how I think about hiring.

[2-3 paragraphs telling the story with professional context]

The lesson: [one-sentence takeaway]

Full conversation on [Podcast Name]: [link in comments]
```

**Lesson posts**: Extract a framework or model discussed in the episode:
```
On this week's episode, @Name broke down their 3-layer approach to content strategy:

1. Audience layer: [brief explanation]
2. Angle layer: [brief explanation]
3. Amplification layer: [brief explanation]

The part that surprised me: [your commentary]
```

#### For Newsletter

**Episode summary**: A curated summary with your highlights and commentary. Not a transcript -- your editorial perspective on what mattered most.

**Pull quotes**: 2-3 standout quotes formatted as visual callouts in the newsletter.

### Step 4: Create an Audiogram Plan

For audio/video clips on social media, identify:

- **Clip timestamps**: [Start] - [End] for each clip (aim for 30-60 seconds)
- **Caption**: The key quote or insight from the clip
- **Visual treatment**: Speaker photo, waveform, or caption overlay
- **Platform**: Square (1:1) for X and LinkedIn, vertical (9:16) for Instagram/TikTok

Aim for 2-3 audiogram clips per episode.

### Step 5: Schedule the Rollout

Do not post everything at once. Spread podcast promotion across the week:

| Day | Post Type | Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Release day | Episode announcement with best quote | X + LinkedIn |
| Day +1 | Audiogram clip #1 | X |
| Day +2 | Story/lesson post from the episode | LinkedIn |
| Day +3 | Thread expanding on one topic from the episode | X |
| Day +4 | Audiogram clip #2 | X |
| Day +5 | Community post in relevant subreddit or Discord | Reddit/Community |

---

## Guest Collaboration

When the podcast has a guest, coordinate promotion:

- **Share draft posts with the guest** for review before publishing
- **Tag the guest** in posts (coordinate on preferred handles)
- **Provide the guest with ready-to-post content** they can share on their own channels
- **Cross-promote**: When the guest shares, engage with their posts to amplify reach

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **"New episode out" posts**: Just announcing that an episode exists is not compelling content. Lead with an insight, quote, or story from the episode.
2. **Transcribing instead of extracting**: Long transcript excerpts do not work on social media. Extract and sharpen.
3. **Ignoring the guest's audience**: The guest brings their own audience. Create content that appeals to both your audience and theirs.
4. **One-and-done promotion**: A single "new episode" post reaches a fraction of your audience. Spread multiple posts across the week.
5. **No visual variety**: Alternate between text posts, audiograms, quote images, and threaded content. Do not use the same format for every promotional post.
