# Voice Integration Guide

This document explains how to apply the brand's voice profile (from `brand/VOICE.md`) to blog post content.

---

## Step 1: Read the Voice Profile

Before writing, read `brand/VOICE.md` and extract:

- **Voice attributes**: The 3-5 adjectives that define the brand's tone (e.g., "direct, witty, technical, warm")
- **Formality level**: Where the brand sits on the spectrum from casual to formal
- **Humor type**: Dry, self-deprecating, playful, none, or situational
- **Sentence rhythm**: Short and punchy, long and flowing, or mixed
- **Signature patterns**: Recurring structural habits (e.g., always opens with a question, uses footnotes for humor, favors numbered lists)
- **Vocabulary preferences**: Words the brand favors and words it avoids
- **Perspective**: First person singular, first person plural ("we"), second person ("you"), or third person

If `brand/VOICE.md` does not exist, use these defaults:
- Voice: Clear, direct, conversational-professional
- Formality: Mid-range (not stiff, not slangy)
- Humor: Light and situational
- Rhythm: Mixed sentence lengths
- Perspective: Second person ("you") for tutorials, first person for opinions
- Suggest the user configure their brand voice for better results

---

## Step 2: Calibrate Before Writing

Before starting the draft, write three test sentences in the brand's voice about the blog topic. Check:

- Do they match the formality level?
- Do they use the right vocabulary?
- Does the rhythm feel right?
- Would someone familiar with the brand recognize the voice?

Adjust until the test sentences feel calibrated, then proceed to the full draft.

---

## Step 3: Apply During Drafting

While writing, keep these voice elements active:

### Formality
- **Formal**: Avoid contractions, use complete phrases, maintain professional distance
- **Mid-range**: Contractions are fine, address the reader directly, occasional colloquialisms
- **Casual**: Contractions always, fragments allowed, conversational asides, personality-forward

### Humor
- **Dry**: Understatement, deadpan observations, irony delivered without signaling
- **Self-deprecating**: Acknowledge your own mistakes or limitations with a light touch
- **Playful**: Wordplay, surprising analogies, unexpected comparisons
- **None**: Straightforward, no jokes or cleverness, let the content speak
- **Situational**: Humor only when it naturally arises from the subject matter

### Sentence Rhythm
- Match the brand's preferred cadence
- If the brand favors short sentences: keep most under 15 words, punch key points in 5-8 word sentences
- If the brand favors longer sentences: use subordinate clauses, build ideas across phrases
- If mixed: alternate deliberately between short and long

### Vocabulary
- Use the brand's preferred terms (check VOICE.md word lists)
- Avoid blacklisted words or phrases
- When in doubt, favor clarity over cleverness

---

## Step 4: Post-Draft Voice Check

After completing the draft, run this verification:

1. **Random paragraph test**: Pick three paragraphs at random. Read each one. Does it sound like the brand, or does it sound generic?

2. **Vocabulary scan**: Search for any words on the brand's avoid-list. Replace them.

3. **Formality consistency**: Check that the formality level doesn't drift (common problem: posts start casual and get progressively more formal, or vice versa).

4. **Humor audit**: If the brand uses humor, is it present? If the brand avoids humor, did any sneak in?

5. **Perspective check**: Is the chosen perspective (I/we/you) consistent throughout?

6. **Signature patterns**: Does the post include at least one of the brand's signature structural patterns?

---

## Step 5: Final Voice Polish

Make a final pass focused only on voice:

- Read the opening paragraph aloud (mentally). Does it immediately establish the brand's tone?
- Check transitions between sections. Do they maintain voice, or do they revert to generic connecting phrases?
- Read the closing. Does it end in the brand's voice, or does it trail off into boilerplate?

---

## When Voice Conflicts with Clarity

If the brand voice would make a passage less clear, prioritize clarity. A slightly off-voice sentence that communicates perfectly is better than a perfectly voiced sentence that confuses the reader. Note the trade-off and move on.

---

## Adapting Voice to Post Type

Different post types may require slight voice adjustments:

| Post Type | Voice Adaptation |
|---|---|
| Tutorial | Slightly more instructional; favor "you" and direct commands |
| Opinion | More personality; the individual author's voice can come forward |
| Case Study | More measured; let the results speak, reduce editorial voice |
| Listicle | Punchier; each item needs to deliver value quickly |
| Deep Dive | More technical; vocabulary can be more specialized, but rhythm still matters |

These are adjustments, not overrides. The core brand voice should remain recognizable across all post types.
